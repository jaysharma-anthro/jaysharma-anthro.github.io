About

I am a sociocultural anthropologist working at the intersections of development, extraction, Indigenous politics, bureaucracy, citizenship, and environmental governance.

I received my PhD in Anthropology from Syracuse University. My research is based on long-term ethnographic fieldwork among Ho Adivasi communities in Jharkhand, eastern India, as well as engagement with colonial and postcolonial histories of administration, caste, and nation-state formation.

Before doctoral study, I trained in sociology and development studies and worked on livelihood and public-policy research in India. This interdisciplinary background informs my interest in connecting close ethnographic analysis to wider debates about governance, political economy, and democratic life.
