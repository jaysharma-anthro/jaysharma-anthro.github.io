Jay Prakash Sharma

Research

My scholarship investigates how extractive development, welfare governance, and environmental transformation reshape relational personhood and political life among Ho Adivasi communities in Jharkhand, eastern India.

Afterlives of Extraction

My book project moves beyond treating extraction only as an economic process or a singular event of dispossession. It examines extraction as a social and political condition that continues to reorganize everyday life: aspirations, kinship, ethical obligations, attachments to land, encounters with officials, and understandings of citizenship.

Bureaucratic Indifference

This research reconceptualizes bureaucratic indifference as a historically sedimented mode of governance. It asks how caste-inflected administrative reasoning and cultural stereotypes allow exclusion to be normalized even when officials perform neutrality and procedural detachment.

Political Becoming

My work on anti-mining mobilization approaches protest as a generative and pedagogical arena. It traces how participation cultivates rights-consciousness, political judgment, and new vernacular forms of leadership, including locally recognized figures described as Naya Neta.

Future Research

A developing project follows Ho Adivasi migrants into urban labor settings, examining how mobility, precarity, and changing relations to land reshape belonging, aspiration, and collective life.
