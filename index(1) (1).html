Jay Prakash Sharma

I am a sociocultural anthropologist whose research examines the intersections of development, extraction, bureaucracy, democracy, and Indigenous politics in contemporary India. Drawing on long-term ethnographic fieldwork among Ho Adivasi communities in eastern India, my work explores how extractive histories continue to shape citizenship, personhood, political subjectivity, and everyday engagements with the state.

Postdoctoral Research Fellow

Current Book Project

Afterlives of Extraction

My research examines how extractive development and welfare governance reshape personhood, political subjectivity, and citizenship among Ho Adivasi communities in eastern India.

The book traces how mining persists beyond the immediate site of extraction, reorganizing relationships to land, labor, kinship, authority, and the state while generating new forms of political imagination and democratic practice.

Current areas of research

Afterlives of Extraction

How mining reorganizes everyday ethical life, aspirations, interpersonal relations, and attachments to territory.

Bureaucracy and Citizenship
